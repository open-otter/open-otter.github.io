# Running Instructions

This page is the starter runbook for powered testing. It is meant to guide operators through a cautious first bring-up, not to replace subsystem-specific procedures or a site-specific safety review.

## Operating assumptions

These instructions assume:

- the chassis is mechanically complete
- the app and firmware have already been built and loaded
- the operator has physical access to the vehicle and a way to remove power quickly
- testing begins at low speed in a controlled area

## Pre-run safety check

Before each session:

- inspect the vehicle for loose wiring, damaged tires, or blocked steering
- verify the battery is secured and charged appropriately
- keep the drive wheels clear or the chassis elevated for the first live-control checks
- confirm there is a clear emergency stop path, including unplugging propulsion power if needed

Do not begin autonomous or assisted driving tests around traffic, pets, or bystanders who are not part of the session.

## Bring-up order

Use a repeatable startup sequence so issues are easier to isolate:

1. power and inspect the vehicle hardware
2. boot the brain device and open the control app
3. power the MCP and confirm it is advertising as `OPENOTTER-MCP`
4. connect from the app and verify the control link is healthy
5. validate neutral steering and throttle behavior before any motion test

## Control and telemetry checks

Before moving under load, confirm:

- BLE connection is stable
- steering commands move in the expected direction
- throttle returns to neutral when commands stop
- any status or safety indicators show a healthy system state

If the vehicle does not return to a neutral state reliably, stop and debug before further testing.

## First motion tests

Start with the smallest practical test:

- low-speed forward and steering response
- controlled stop
- repeatability across reconnects or short pauses

Only expand to larger runs after the basic loop is predictable. Reverse motion deserves extra caution if rear-distance safety logic is part of the current build.

### Safety notes

- Never begin with full-throttle tests.
- Keep hands, tools, and loose clothing away from rotating parts.
- Re-check neutral behavior after firmware updates, app updates, or wiring changes.

## Shutdown and post-run review

When the session is over:

1. stop the vehicle and return controls to neutral
2. disconnect the control session
3. remove propulsion power
4. inspect for heat, loose hardware, or wiring damage
5. record any failures before the next run
